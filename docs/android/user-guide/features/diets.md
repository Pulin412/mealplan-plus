# Diets

A **diet** is a full-day meal template. It groups your meals into slots (Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner, etc.) and defines your nutritional targets for a day. Once created, you can assign a diet to any date on your calendar.

---

## Why Use Diets?

Instead of planning meals from scratch every day, you create reusable diet templates:
- **Remission Diet** — a stricter plan for active diabetes management
- **Maintenance Diet** — a balanced everyday plan
- **Cheat Day** — a relaxed plan for weekends

Assign a diet to a date and your plan is set for the day.

---

## Browsing Diets

Go to the **Diets** tab. Each diet card shows:
- Diet name and description
- Total daily calories
- Macro totals (protein / carbs / fat)
- Number of meals assigned
- Tags (coloured chips)
- Food previews

---

## Creating a Diet

1. Tap **+** on the Diets screen.
2. Enter a **name** and optional **description**.
3. Tap **Save** — you are taken to the Diet Detail screen.
4. Tap any meal slot (Breakfast, Lunch, etc.) to assign a meal:
   - Browse your meals or search by name.
   - Select a meal → it is assigned to that slot.
5. Repeat for each slot you want to fill.

Unassigned slots are shown as empty — that is fine, not every slot needs a meal.

---

## Diet Detail Screen

Shows all slots and their assigned meals with macro totals. From here you can:
- Tap a slot to replace its meal.
- Tap a meal name to view its food contents (read-only).
- See the diet's total daily nutrition at a glance.
- Edit the diet name/description.
- Add or manage tags.

---

## Tags

Tags help you categorise and filter diets. Examples:
- `Remission` (red) — for strict management periods
- `Maintenance` (green) — everyday balanced plan
- `High Protein` (blue)
- `SOS` (orange) — emergency low-carb fallback

To add a tag: open a diet → tap **Tags** → select from existing tags or create a new one with a colour.

---

## Deleting a Diet

Open the diet → tap the **delete icon**. Diets assigned to calendar dates are unlinked but the logged data for those dates is preserved.

---

## Diet Summary on the Diets Screen

Each diet card shows a summary row:
- **Foods** — comma-separated list of key ingredients across all meals
- **Slots** — which meal slots are covered (B / MS / L / ES / D)
- **Macros** — total daily protein, carbs, fat in grams

This lets you compare diets at a glance without opening each one.

---

## System vs Custom Diets

The app ships with a few **system diets** as examples (marked with a chip). These can be viewed but not edited. Duplicate a system diet to create your own editable version.
