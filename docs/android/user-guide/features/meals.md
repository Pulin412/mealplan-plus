# Meals

A **meal** is a named collection of foods with specified quantities. Meals are reusable templates — create "Breakfast Bowl" once, then use it in multiple diets or log it on multiple days.

---

## Viewing Your Meals

Go to **Home → Meals**. Each meal card shows:
- Meal name
- Total calories
- Macro breakdown (protein / carbs / fat)
- Number of food items

---

## Creating a Meal

1. Tap the **+** button on the Meals screen.
2. Enter a **meal name** (e.g. "Oats with Milk", "Grilled Chicken Salad").
3. Tap **Add Food** to add items:
   - Search your food database.
   - Select a food, set the quantity and unit.
   - Tap **Add**.
4. Repeat for all foods in the meal.
5. Tap **Save**.

The meal total (calories, protein, carbs, fat) is calculated automatically from all added foods.

---

## Editing a Meal

Tap any meal → tap the **edit (pencil) icon**.
- Add or remove food items.
- Change quantities or units.
- Rename the meal.

Changes apply everywhere the meal is referenced (diets, logs).

---

## Deleting a Meal

Tap any meal → tap **Delete**. Meals that are assigned to a diet template cannot be deleted until removed from those diets.

---

## Meal Slots

When assigning a meal to a diet, you assign it to a **slot** (meal occasion):

| Slot | Typical time |
|------|-------------|
| Breakfast | Morning |
| Morning Snack | Mid-morning |
| Lunch | Midday |
| Evening Snack | Afternoon |
| Dinner | Evening |

You can also create **custom slots** per day in the Daily Log (e.g. "Post-workout shake").

---

## Tips

- Keep meals **granular** — a "meal" should represent one eating occasion, not the whole day. That way you can mix and match them in different diets.
- Name meals descriptively: "Oats 50g + Milk 200ml" is more useful than "Breakfast".
- Use the **Food Picker** search to quickly find foods by partial name.
