# Health Metrics

The **Health** tab lets you log and visualise health measurements over time. Track weight, blood glucose, blood pressure, or any custom metric you define.

---

## Built-in Metric Types

| Metric | Unit | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| Weight | kg / lbs | Body weight |
| Blood Glucose | mmol/L or mg/dL | With sub-types: Fasting, Post-meal, Random |
| Blood Pressure | mmHg | Systolic/Diastolic |
| HbA1c | % | Long-term glucose average |

---

## Custom Metric Types

You can create your own metric types for anything not covered above:

1. Go to **Health** → tap **Manage Types**.
2. Tap **+** to create a new type.
3. Enter:
   - **Name** (e.g. "Waist Circumference")
   - **Unit** (e.g. "cm")
   - Optional **min/max** range for normal values
4. Tap **Save**.

The custom type now appears in the list when logging a new metric.

---

## Logging a Measurement

1. Go to the **Health** tab.
2. Tap **+**.
3. Select the metric type.
4. Enter the value.
5. For **Blood Glucose**, also select the sub-type:
   - **Fasting** — measured first thing in the morning before eating
   - **Post-meal** — measured 1-2 hours after eating
   - **Random** — measured at any other time
6. Set the date and time (defaults to now).
7. Tap **Save**.

---

## Viewing History

The Health screen shows a chronological list of all logged metrics. Tap any entry to edit or delete it.

Filter by metric type using the chips at the top of the list.

---

## Charts

Tap **View Charts** to see your metrics visualised over time.

- Line charts for continuous metrics (weight, blood glucose).
- Select the metric type and date range using the controls at the top.
- Trend lines show the direction of change over time.
- Normal range bands are shown when min/max values are set for a metric type.

---

## Blood Glucose Tips

- Log **Fasting** glucose every morning before breakfast.
- Log **Post-meal** glucose 2 hours after a meal to see the impact of specific foods.
- Use **Charts** to see patterns — e.g. does your lunch diet cause a spike?

Correlate your glucose readings with your food logs to identify which meals or foods affect your levels most.

---

## Exporting Health Data

Go to **Settings → Export Data** to export your health metrics as a CSV file. This can be shared with your doctor or imported into a spreadsheet.
